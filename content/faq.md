*Based on the statutes and Admission Guidelines. Some operational details (membership fees, online sign-up) will be confirmed by the Executive Board and the General Assembly.*

## Who can become a member?
Any individual who supports the aims of the association (Statutes §4(1)).
Researchers, practitioners, and students across all regions and disciplines are
welcome — international participation is intended by design.

## How do I join?
Membership applications are made in writing (including electronically) to the
Executive Board, which decides on admission (§4(3)). Once the website is running,
there will be a short online sign-up form.

## What types of membership are there?
Membership is for **individuals** (institutional or company memberships are not
offered). The association distinguishes members **with** and **without** voting
rights. Unless the Executive Board decides otherwise at admission, **new members
are voting members by default**.

- **Voting members** — paying members with full rights. They may:
  - take part in the General Assembly,
  - vote,
  - stand for election to the Executive Board and other elected bodies,
  - receive all member benefits.

- **Reduced-fee members** (students and early-career researchers) — a reduced rate
  applies (see fees), kept as **voting members** with the same rights.

- **Non-voting members** — admitted by the Executive Board where participation is
  mainly for scientific exchange, advisory involvement, or support, without taking
  part in formal governance. They do **not** vote or stand for office. The board
  decides whether they pay a fee.

In short: most members are voting members; a reduced rate may be added later; and
non-voting is a special category for advisory/supporting roles.

## I attended the founding meeting and signed the documents. Do I need to sign up again?
No. You are already a **founding member** — you do not re-apply. Once the
membership system is live, you will simply complete a short online profile (and
pay dues once a fee schedule is set), so the board has a clean member register.
This is a formality for record-keeping, not a second admission.

## Are there membership fees?
Fees are set by the **General Assembly** in a fee schedule (§4a). The proposed
schedule — **pending approval by the General Assembly** — is:

| Category | 1 year | 2 years | 5 years |
|----------|--------|---------|---------|
| Regular | €50 | €90 | €200 |
| Student & early-career | €20 | €36 | €80 |

- Membership runs **from the day you join**.
- Paying **2 or 5 years upfront is discounted** (the 5-year rate is four years' fee).
- Members may receive a discount on PED/TGF registration — **none at PED27**, and
  a discount of **up to ~5% from TGF28 onward**, agreed per conference.

The board may also reduce or waive fees on justified request (§4a). Final amounts
will be confirmed by the General Assembly.

## What is expected of me as a member?
Support the association's aims, follow the Code of Conduct, and pay dues once set.
Holding an office is **not** required. Members are warmly encouraged to help the
community grow — invite colleagues and spread the word.

## Can I promote IACD and recruit members on its behalf?
Yes — every member is an ambassador. You may promote IACD, share announcements,
and invite colleagues to join. One distinction: *official representation* (binding
statements or commitments in the association's name) is legally reserved to the
Executive Board (two board members jointly, §6(3)). For official text or
commitments, coordinate with the board; informal advocacy is open to everyone.

## How is the Steering Committee chosen?
- The **General Assembly (all voting members)** elects or confirms the Steering
  Committee — not the board alone (§7(5), §11(2)).
- **Nominations** may come from members or from the board; the General Assembly
  then votes (simple majority).
- It may include **both association members and external experts**; board members
  may also serve (§11(2)).
- **Size: 5–30 members. Term: 2 years**, reappointment allowed (§11(3), Admission
  Guidelines).
- Composition reflects **geographic, disciplinary, and gender diversity**; members
  should generally have taken part in PED or TGF (§11(2)).

## What does the Steering Committee do?
It provides strategic and scientific guidance for the PED and TGF conferences —
selection of organizing teams, scientific direction, and the composition of
scientific committees (§11(4)). The Executive Board decides on conference hosting
rights based on its recommendations.

## What bodies does IACD have?
- **General Assembly** — all members; highest decision-making body.
- **Executive Board** — Chair, Deputy Chair, Treasurer; runs day-to-day business.
- **Steering Committee** — scientific advisory body for PED and TGF.
